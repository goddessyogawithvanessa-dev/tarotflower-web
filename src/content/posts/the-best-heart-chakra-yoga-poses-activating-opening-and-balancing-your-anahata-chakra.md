### 1. Eagle Arms

https://youtube.com/shorts/robN1riN7fY?feature=share

Here is a step-by-step guide to doing the Yoga Eagle Arms Pose:

1. Begin by sitting in a comfortable position with your spine straight.
2. Lift your arms to shoulder height, parallel to the ground, with your palms facing down.
3. Cross your right arm over your left arm at the elbow, bringing your palms together.
4. Lift your elbows to shoulder height and press your forearms and hands together.
5. Press your shoulders and arms down and away from your ears and feel your back opening.
6. Keep your chest open and your shoulders relaxed.
7. Hold the pose for 5-10 breaths, then release and repeat on the other side by crossing your left arm over your right arm.

Remember to listen to your body and only go as far as feels comfortable for you. Enjoy the stretch and the benefits it brings to your upper body and mind.

### 2. Cat/Cow

https://youtube.com/shorts/\_lkjp77K0zc?feature=share

Here is how to practice the cat/cow poses (Marjaryasana/Bitilasana):

1. Start on your hands and knees with your wrists under your shoulders and knees under your hips.
2. Inhale and initiate by lifting your tailbone toward the ceiling. As your belly lowers and your spine bends your head also reaches toward the ceiling creating a beautiful crescent shape with your back like a wave. This is the "cow" portion of the pose.
3. Exhale, leading with your tailbone as you invert the pose. Your navel presses up through your core towards the ceiling, as you tuck your chin to your chest, and drawing your tailbone towards your knees for the "cat" portion of the pose.
4. Repeat this flow of movements, inhaling to cow and exhaling to cat, for a few rounds while syncing your breath with your movements.
5. You can also add variations such as circling your hips, extending one arm or leg at a time, or pausing in either pose for a deeper stretch.

Remember to maintain good alignment throughout the pose, keeping your core engaged, your shoulders away from your ears, and your gaze forward. Enjoy the gentle movement and the benefits of this soothing pose!

### 3. Puppy Pose

https://youtube.com/shorts/gwOpnVYSCcI?feature=share

Here is how to practice the Puppy Pose or Uttana Shishosana:

1. Start on your hands and knees with your wrists under your shoulders and knees under your hips.
2. Walk your hands forward, keeping your arms straight, until your forehead touches the ground and your chest is lowered towards the mat.
3. Extend your arms forward with palms down and elbows lifted off the ground to stretch your shoulders and upper back.
4. Stay in the position for a few breaths.
5. Slowly walk your hands back towards your knees to come out of the pose.
6. Repeat the sequence a few times while syncing your breath with your movements.

Remember to keep your core engaged, your hips over your knees, and your gaze down towards the ground for good alignment. If you have any wrist or shoulder injuries, avoid this pose. Enjoy the gentle stretch and the benefits of this soothing pose!

### 4. Cobra Pose

https://youtube.com/shorts/imA92Rt-DD0?feature=share

The Cobra Pose, or Bhujangasana in Sanskrit. Here are the steps to perform the pose:

1. Start by lying flat on your stomach. Place your hands, palms down on the floor by your sides, so that your fingertips are aligned with the tops of your shoulders. Keep your feet together.
2. Place your elbows close to your sides, and keep them bent at a 90-degree angle as you slowly press up.
3. Inhale deeply and slowly lift your head, chest, and torso off the mat while keeping your elbows close to your sides. Use your back muscles to lift yourself up, not your arms.
4. Straighten your arms and lift your chest further. Make sure your shoulders are relaxed and not hunched up towards your ears.
5. Keep your gaze forward and hold the pose for a few breaths. You can also gently tilt your head back to deepen the stretch.
6. To release the pose, exhale and slowly lower your torso back down to the mat.
7. Repeat the pose for 3-5 times, taking deep breaths in each repetition.

Tips:

* Make sure to engage your core muscles to protect your lower back.
* Don't overdo the stretch or strain your neck by looking too far back.
* Modify the pose by coming up only halfway if you have back pain or discomfort.

### 5. Fish Pose

https://youtube.com/shorts/dQisvCna2AM?feature=share

Here are the steps to practicing the Fish Pose, also called Matsyasana:

1. Begin by laying on your back
2. Slide your hands under your hips, palms down.
3. Keep your arms and elbows attached to your sides
4. Inhale and lift your chest towards the ceiling, pressing down through your elbows and forearms. Tilt your head back and rest the crown of your head on the ground behind you.
5. Hold the position for a few breaths.
6. Exhale, release your head back to the ground, and slowly lower your chest and arms back down to the mat.
7. Repeat the sequence a few times, syncing your breath with your movements.

Remember to maintain good alignment throughout the pose, keeping your core engaged, your shoulders away from your ears, and your gaze towards the ceiling or behind you. Also, be sure to avoid this pose if you have any neck or back injuries. Enjoy the gentle stretch and the benefits of this soothing pose!
