The Wheel of Fortune tarot card represents change and good luck! Just like the seasons, our lives move in cycles which cannot be controlled. Surrender to change and enjoy your new beginning when the Wheel of Fortune shows up in your tarot card reading! These changes are far beyond your control.

Representing the cycles of life, destiny, and the ever-turning wheel of fate, this card carries profound symbolism. With the number 10, it signifies completion and transition, marking the end of one phase and the beginning of another. The Wheel of Fortune is closely associated with the planet Jupiter and the element of Fire.

As the wheel turns, it brings forth the unpredictable nature of life and the constant ebb and flow of circumstances. This card symbolizes the interplay between chance and destiny, reminding us that life is full of ups and downs, victories and setbacks. The Wheel of Fortune teaches us the importance of adapting to change, embracing the opportunities that come our way, and finding balance amidst the ever-changing tides. It serves as a reminder that nothing is permanent and that we have the power to shape our own destiny through our choices and actions.

The Wheel of Fortune carries several key characteristics that guide its interpretation. It embodies the concept of karma, highlighting the consequences of our past actions and the potential for future rewards or challenges. This card signifies a turning point or a pivotal moment in life, where unexpected events or circumstances can alter the course of our journey. It also emphasizes the importance of being open to new experiences and taking calculated risks, as the wheel continues to spin, offering us the chance for growth, expansion, and personal evolution.

## How to Embody The Wheel of Fortune Tarot Card

<p><img src="/images/2021/01/wheel.jpg" alt="Wheel of Fortune Tarot artwork for How to Embody The Wheel of Fortune Tarot Card" width="1024" height="1024" loading="lazy" decoding="async" /></p>

Embodying the lessons of the Wheel of Fortune Tarot card involves embracing the following practices:

1. **Embrace Change:** Recognize that change is an inevitable part of life and approach it with an open mind and a sense of curiosity. Embrace the opportunities that come with each turn of the wheel, even if they bring uncertainty or challenge.
2. **Trust in Divine Timing:** Cultivate trust in the timing of the Universe and have faith that everything unfolds as it should. Practice surrendering the need for control and allow the natural flow of life to guide you. Trust that the wheel is turning in your favor, even during challenging times.
3. **Adaptability and Flexibility:** Develop the ability to adapt to changing circumstances and be flexible in your approach. Stay open to new possibilities and be willing to adjust your plans or perspectives as needed. This will help you navigate the ups and downs of life with greater ease.
4. **Release Attachment to Outcomes:** Let go of rigid expectations and attachments to specific outcomes. Instead, focus on the present moment and the journey itself, rather than fixating on a predetermined destination. By releasing attachment, you free yourself from unnecessary limitations and allow space for new experiences.
5. **Embrace Opportunities:** Be open to new opportunities that present themselves along your path. Take calculated risks and step out of your comfort zone when necessary. The Wheel of Fortune teaches that fortune favors the bold, so seize the chances that come your way.
6. **Practice Gratitude:** Cultivate a mindset of gratitude for both the highs and lows of your journey. Recognize that every experience, whether perceived as positive or negative, contributes to your growth and evolution. Embracing gratitude helps you maintain a positive outlook and invites more blessings into your life.

By embodying these practices, you align yourself with the energy and wisdom of the Wheel of Fortune Tarot card. Embrace the ever-turning wheel of life, trusting in its cycles, and find joy in the journey as you navigate the twists and turns with grace and resilience.

## The Wheel of Fortune Tarot Card in the Major Arcana

<p><img src="/images/2021/01/wheel-4.jpg" alt="Wheel of Fortune Tarot artwork for The Wheel of Fortune Tarot Card in the Major Arcana" width="1024" height="1024" loading="lazy" decoding="async" /></p>

The Wheel of Fortune Tarot card holds a significant place in the Major Arcana, as it connects with the cards that come before and after it, offering a deeper understanding of its meaning.

In relation to the card before it, the Wheel of Fortune follows the [Justice](/justice-tarot) card. This transition suggests a karmic shift or a sense of balance being restored. After making fair and just decisions, the Wheel of Fortune signifies the consequences and outcomes that follow. It reminds us that our actions and choices have a direct impact on the wheel of life, and we must accept the repercussions of our decisions.

As for the card that follows the Wheel of Fortune, it is the [Strength](/strength-tarot) card. This progression represents the power to navigate and influence the wheel of destiny. After experiencing the unpredictable turns of the Wheel of Fortune, Strength teaches us to find inner courage and resilience. It encourages us to use our strength and inner fortitude to embrace the changes that come our way, enabling us to ride the wheel with grace and determination.

Together, these three cards form a transformative sequence. The Justice card highlights the importance of making fair decisions, leading us to the Wheel of Fortune where we face the consequences of those choices. And with the strength gained from this experience, we move forward, prepared to face whatever challenges and opportunities lie ahead. It reminds us that life is a cyclical journey, and each turn of the wheel offers a chance for growth, self-discovery, and embracing the ever-changing nature of our existence.

## The Wheel of Fortune Tarot Card in the Rider Waite

<p><img src="/images/2021/01/Rider-Waite-8.jpg" alt="Wheel of Fortune card from the Rider-Waite Tarot" width="540" height="540" loading="lazy" decoding="async" /></p>

The Wheel of Fortune Tarot card in the Rider-Waite deck is rich in symbolism, reflecting the cyclical nature of life and the ever-turning wheel of destiny.

At the center of the card, we see a large wheel, representing the Wheel of Fortune itself. It is divided into different sections, reminiscent of the four elements or the four seasons, reminding us of the constant changes and cycles we experience. The wheel is surrounded by a blue sky, symbolizing clarity and possibilities.

On the wheel, there are three creatures: a human, a lion, and a sphinx. These creatures represent different aspects of existence. The human figure signifies humanity and our ability to adapt to the changing circumstances of life. The lion represents strength, courage, and our primal instincts. The sphinx embodies wisdom and the mysteries of the universe.

The Wheel of Fortune also features a snake, representing the eternal cycle of birth, death, and rebirth. The snake biting its tail forms an ouroboros, symbolizing the infinite nature of time and the interconnectedness of all things.

At the top of the card, we find the Egyptian god Anubis, the guardian of the afterlife. His presence suggests that the wheel's turning is linked to the larger forces of fate and destiny.

Overall, the Wheel of Fortune's symbolism in the Rider-Waite deck reminds us that life is constantly changing, and our fortunes can shift in unexpected ways. It calls us to embrace the ebb and flow of existence, to find balance amidst the ups and downs, and to recognize that we have the power to navigate and influence our own destinies as we move along the wheel of life.

### The Symbolism of the Serpent on the Wheel of Fortune Card

The idea behind the Serpent representation is similar to the Ancient African belief. For them, the Serpent is a highly valued creature. It refers to fortune. We can see this reference in the entire African culture with the [Twa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twa), the African Druids and high-ranked officials of their governments wearing the Uraeus as a headpiece.

Because of this and also for its ability to shed its own skin, the snake was a symbol of spiritual power, birth and rebirth and, by extension, immortality. It is the beginning of the human journey as he or she enters this plane of existence.
