## The Chariot Tarot Meaning

![Sagittarius zodiac, spirit animal, the horse, Midjourney art by Vanessa Hylande](/images/2023/05/vanessahylande_a_gorgeous_bejeweled_powerful_white_horse_gallop_29bc16d9-0e2d-48dc-8cc1-2be4b7be4f1d.jpg)

The Chariot Tarot card is a powerful symbol of determination and willpower. It represents a journey of self-mastery and harnessing one's inner strength to overcome obstacles and achieve success. The Chariot calls us to take control of our lives and steer ourselves towards our desired destination.

At its core, the Chariot signifies the need for balance and harmony. It urges us to find equilibrium between opposing forces, whether it be the conscious and unconscious mind, external influences and internal desires, or the different aspects of our own personalities. The Chariot reminds us that true victory comes when we can navigate through life's challenges with a balanced perspective and a clear sense of purpose.

This card is a symbol of forward movement and progress. It encourages us to embrace our ambitions and take decisive action. The Chariot reminds us that we have the power and the drive to achieve our goals. It calls upon our inner strength and determination, urging us to trust in our abilities and face any obstacles with courage and resilience.

In a reading, the Chariot often represents a period of transformation and personal growth. It indicates a time when we must take control of our lives and assert ourselves to move forward. The Chariot encourages us to maintain focus, maintain our direction, and stay true to our path, even in the face of uncertainty. It reminds us that we have the capacity to overcome challenges, achieve victory, and become the masters of our own destinies.

## The Chariot Tarot Card In the Major Arcana

![the chariot tarot card meaning, major arcana, the chariot card, Midjourney art by Vanessa Hylande](/images/2022/02/chariot-3.jpg)

The Chariot card holds a significant position in the [Major Arcana](https://tarotflower.com/major-arcana-2/), and its placement in relation to the cards before and after it provides insights into its role in the spiritual journey. Preceding the Chariot is [The Lovers](https://tarotflower.com/tarot-card-meanings/the-lovers-tarot/), which represents the exploration of love, choices, and partnerships. The Chariot builds upon the lessons learned in The Lovers and signifies the need to take decisive action and harness inner strength to navigate through challenges and conflicts.

Following the Chariot is [Strength](https://tarotflower.com/tarot-card-meanings/strength-tarot/), which symbolizes inner courage, resilience, and the ability to tame the wild aspects of our nature. The Chariot's energy prepares us for the transformative power of Strength by teaching us the importance of asserting control, focusing our intentions, and maintaining a balanced approach. It sets the stage for developing inner fortitude and unwavering determination to overcome obstacles on the path to personal growth and self-mastery.

In this context, the Chariot acts as a bridge between the exploration of relationships and the cultivation of inner strength. It signifies the transition from external influences to internal empowerment, highlighting the need to assert one's will and take charge of personal destiny. Together with The Lovers and Strength, the Chariot forms a progression that encourages individuals to make conscious choices, overcome challenges, and tap into their inner power to manifest their desires.

Overall, the Chariot's placement between The Lovers and Strength reflects the journey from external connections to internal empowerment, underscoring the importance of balancing relationships with the cultivation of personal strength and determination. It signifies the transformative process of taking control of one's life, embracing challenges, and moving forward with confidence on the path of self-realization.

## The Chariot Card in the Rider Waite

[![the chariot tarot card meaning, major arcana, rider waite](/images/2022/02/Rider-Waite-9.jpg)](https://amzn.to/3N3MrON)

In the traditional [Rider-Waite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rider%E2%80%93Waite_tarot_deck) tarot deck, the charioteer is grasping the reins of two sphinxes - one black and one white. They represent opposing forces, the balancing of polar opposites. But The Chariot tarot card shows that he has made it to his destination. He has learned how to maneuver and direct the energies toward a single purpose.

He is covered by a canopy of stars and wears a crescent moon on his shoulder. These symbols represent the spiritual and celestial influences that are guiding him. He also wears crown of laurels and stars upon his head, which represents his victory.

The black and white sphinxes represent the duality of positive and negative. This is also reminiscent of the two pillars of [The High Priestess tarot](https://tarotflower.com/tarot-card-meanings/the-high-priestess-tarot/) card. The King's six-pointed star crown, which is represents not just victory, but also success and spiritual evolution. The two crescent moons also symbolize what is coming into being. With a scepter in his right hand like [The Magician tarot](https://tarotflower.com/tarot-card-meanings/the-magician-tarot/) card, the King is representative of Divine will. He is calm, tall and proud, and the city behind him is safely in his control.

## The Chariot Card Meaning in the Past Position

[![the chariot tarot card meaning major arcana](/images/2022/02/the-chariot-1024x1006.jpeg)](https://amzn.to/42F9zJt)

The Chariot Tarot card pictured here is from the beautiful [White Sage Tarot Deck](https://amzn.to/42F9zJt)

In the past position, The Chariot tarot card indicates that you have recently completed something significant or ended a long journey successfully. There was significant forward motion that may have included travel. You may have even moved to a new location or job recently.

In any tarot reading, the presentation of the cards is as important because their meaning adjusts depending on where they present in the tarot card spread. When The Chariot is drawn in the past position, it means that you have come from a victorious place. It's the reason you are where you are today. It could also be that this previous success is leaving you wanting for more in your current situation.

Therefore, As with all reversed cards, if this card presents in the past in reverse, then it could be that you have allowed obstacles to discourage you in the past. Did you relinquish your power or lose your determination somewhere along the line? You will have to determine what resonates with you based on the question you are asking throughout the tarot card reading.

## The Chariot Card Meaning in the Present Position

![the chariot tarot card meaning, divine feminine tarot, horse spirit animal, major arcana, Midjourney art by Vanessa Hylande](/images/2022/02/chariot-df1.jpg)

In the present position, a major arcana card like The Chariot indicates that things are about to move forward! If you've been feeling stuck or have been feeling depleted by your efforts to complete a project or objective, take heart. You are about to arrive! You may be required to travel or relocate, whether for work or pleasure. Either way, this major arcana card is a great card to have in the present position, as it indicates advancement and victory!

The Chariot appearing in the present position also indicates that victory is at hand! Whatever you have been fighting and working for is now going to come to fruition. But, always remember that the Chariot tarot card is a card of duality. The charioteer has to remember to manage the opposing forces of the two sphinxes. Have you have achieved the control you desire, but are now at a loss of what to do with that control? Always be honest and grateful for any challenge or struggle you encounter because it will bring you wisdom.

## The Chariot Card Meaning in the Future Position

![chariot tarot card, manifest love, couple](/images/2022/02/4-1.jpg)

In the future position, The Chariot tarot card encourages you to keep going. Forward motion is in the near future, but you are not quite ready at the present moment. Are there loose ends you need to tie up first? Are you needing to learn how to balance opposing forces more effectively in order to reach your destination?

However, the Chariot tarot card appearing in the future position is really fortuitous! It is where the card is at its most powerful. The Chariot tarot card blesses all of the surrounding cards with a message of success and victory. So, don't worry! You're going to be victorious!

As with all major arcana cards, the energies of the Chariot are significant and long term. Take a moment to reflect on what might be standing in your way and how you might better maneuver opposing forces in your favor.

## Your Spirituality Reading and The Chariot Card

In a spirituality reading, The Chariot tarot card speaks of balancing and maneuvering opposing forces. The Charioteer does not use brute strength or manipulation to achieve his goals. Rather, the card teaches us to balance opposing forces, to persevere. Do not be one sided and do not engage in black and white thinking. The dark and the light, the good and the bad, the ease and the difficulty are all parts of one whole. One cannot exist without the other.

## The Chariot Reversed

![chariot tarot card, embody the tarot, embodiment](/images/2022/02/3-1.jpg)

In the reverse, The Chariot tarot card advises you not to move forward with something in this moment. You may be pushing and driving something forward in a way that will backfire. Or the card can indicate that forward motion is currently halted by external forces. A card of travel, The Chariot in reverse suggests that you will be or ought to stay put.

As with all reversed cards, the core qualities of this card are somehow pulled out of balance in your life. Think about it in terms of excess and lack. If the Chariot is in control and determined, one reversed interpretation is that your need to control has become excessive and is actually preventing you from succeeding. Are you pushing ahead at breakneck speed while at the same time disregarding the signs that it’s not working for you? This is an example of The Chariot tarot card being in a state of excess.

### When the Energies of the Chariot are Lacking

On the other hand, another interpretation of any reversed tarot card is one of lack. If the Chariot represents willpower and confidence, maybe you don't have enough of these qualities alive in your inner self at the moment. Are you giving in and giving up? Are you looking at the long journey and struggle ahead and throwing your arms up in the air?

Whenever a card presents in the reverse, we need to analyze the essential meaning of the card and then figure out where we are not in a balanced alignment with these energies. And remember, depending on the querent’s question, this card can always represent someone else in your life.
