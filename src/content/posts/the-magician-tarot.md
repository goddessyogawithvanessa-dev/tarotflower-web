## The Magician in the Major Arcana

![embody the magician tarot card major arcana midjourney art by vanessa hylande](/images/2021/01/magician-3.jpg)

The Magician Tarot card holds a significant place in the sequence of the Tarot deck, appearing as the card after [The Fool](https://tarotflower.com/tarot-card-meanings/the-fool-tarot/) and before [The High Priestess](https://tarotflower.com/tarot-card-meanings/the-high-priestess-tarot/). As the card after the Fool, the Magician represents the transition from innocence and potential to taking action and harnessing one's skills and resources. It symbolizes the realization that you possess the tools and abilities needed to create the life you desire. The Magician brings a sense of purpose and direction after the open-endedness of the Fool's journey.

In relation to the High Priestess, the Magician signifies the practical application of intuitive wisdom. While the High Priestess embodies introspection and spiritual exploration, the Magician represents the ability to translate those insights into tangible results. It signifies a harmonious balance between the intuitive and rational aspects of yourself. The Magician encourages you to integrate your inner wisdom with practical action, combining your knowledge and intuition to manifest your desires in the physical world.

Together, the Magician, the card before, and the High Priestess, the card after, form a powerful triad in the Tarot. They represent the progression from innocence and potential to taking action and utilizing intuitive wisdom. The Magician serves as the bridge between these two cards, embodying the transformative energy that arises from merging the spiritual and the practical.

## Symbology of The Magician

[![the magician tarot card rider waite tarot deck](/images/2021/01/the-magician-canva.jpeg)](https://amzn.to/42KEBP5)

In the [Rider-Waite Tarot deck](https://amzn.to/3LYsF5o), the Magician card is rich in symbolism that represents the power of manifestation and the ability to create change. The central figure in the card is a magician standing at a table adorned with various objects. The magician holds a wand representing action and creativity in one hand, while the other hand points upward, connecting with divine energy.

The symbols on the table—the cup, sword, pentacle, and wand—represent the four elements and the four suits of the Tarot. This signifies the Magician's command over all aspects of life. The infinity symbol above the Magician's head suggests limitless potential and the connection between the spiritual and physical realms. The white and red roses represent purity and passion, and the lemniscate symbolizes eternal existence.

The Magician card invites individuals to harness their inner power, use their skills and resources, and align themselves with the divine forces to manifest their desires. It is a reminder that with focus, intention, and the proper use of one's abilities, one can bring about transformation and create their own reality.
