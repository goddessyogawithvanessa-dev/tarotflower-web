## The Hermit Tarot Card Meaning

![the hermit tarot card meaning, the hermit yes or no, tarot the hermit meaning, Midjourney art by Vanessa Hylande](/images/2022/04/hermit-df-8.jpg)

The Hermit Tarot card, with its associated planet Mercury, zodiac sign Virgo, number 9, and element Earth, serves as a profound symbol of introspection, spiritual growth, and wisdom-seeking. It beckons us to embark on a transformative journey of self-discovery, both internally and externally.

When The Hermit card emerges in a reading, it signals the need to detach from the chaos and distractions of the outside world. It calls for a deliberate retreat to explore the depths of our inner selves. This journey may be lengthy and demanding, often requiring personal sacrifices, yet its significance cannot be overstated. Despite the moments of solitude and potential loneliness that may arise, they hold invaluable lessons to be unraveled.

The Hermit card implores us to engage in meditative practices, establish a profound connection with nature, and immerse ourselves in the exploration of our surroundings. It advises us to persist in our quest for truth and self-understanding, assuring us that the universe wholeheartedly supports our endeavors. By delving into our own depths, we unlock profound insights and tap into the wellspring of wisdom that resides within us. The Hermit reminds us that in embracing this path of self-discovery, we align ourselves with the harmonious rhythms of the cosmos, paving the way for profound personal growth and spiritual enlightenment.

## The Hermit Card in the Major Arcana

![the hermit tarot card meaning, the hermit yes or no, tarot the hermit meaning, Midjourney art by Vanessa Hylande](/images/2022/04/herit-df-4.jpg)

The Hermit tarot card is the **ninth** card in the Major Arcana and represents the querent's journey towards spiritual enlightenment. It follows the [Strength Tarot](https://tarotflower.com/tarot-card-meanings/strength-tarot/) card and precedes the [Wheel of Fortune Tarot](https://tarotflower.com/tarot-card-meanings/the-wheel-of-fortune-tarot/) card, signifying a transitional phase in the querent's spiritual journey.

Before reaching the Hermit card, the querent had to overcome their inner struggles and find the courage to face their fears with the Strength card. The Strength card symbolizes the power of inner strength, courage, and determination, which are all necessary qualities for the querent to embody on their journey towards enlightenment.

After the Hermit card, the Wheel of Fortune card awaits the querent, representing the ups and downs of life's journey. This card reminds the querent to remain adaptable, to embrace change, and to trust that their journey is guided by a higher power.

Overall, the Hermit tarot card signifies the importance of taking time for introspection, reflection, and solitude on the path towards spiritual growth. Through the Hermit's example, the querent can learn to listen to their inner voice, find clarity, and trust the wisdom of their own intuition.

## The Hermit Card Meaning: Rider Waite

![the hermit tarot card meaning major arcana rider waite](/images/2023/05/Rider-Waite.jpg)

The Hermit tarot card in the traditional [Rider Waite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rider%E2%80%93Waite_Tarot) deck features an old man carrying a lantern, standing on top of a mountain. This image symbolizes the idea of going within oneself to seek knowledge and truth. The lantern represents the inner light that guides us through our darkest moments, and the mountain represents the spiritual journey that we must undertake to reach our higher self.

The Hermit is a solitary figure, and his isolation represents the need for introspection and self-reflection. He holds a staff, which symbolizes his wisdom and experience, and the cloak that he wears is gray, symbolizing neutrality and detachment from the material world. The Hermit's face is often hidden, which represents the idea that we must look within ourselves to find answers rather than seeking them from external sources.

In a tarot reading, the Hermit card suggests that it is time to take a step back from the outside world and focus on our inner selves. It can represent a need for solitude, introspection, and self-reflection. This card can also indicate a time of spiritual growth, where we must let go of old beliefs and patterns of behavior to make room for new insights and perspectives. The Hermit encourages us to embrace the unknown and trust in our own inner guidance to lead us towards a greater sense of purpose and meaning in life.
